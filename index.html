Key Insights

What the data actually shows when you compare regions side by side.

🌏
82

Asia Scores Highest

China leads at 82. Indonesia, South Korea (both 75), and Thailand (72) cluster well above 70. East Asia averages 65; SE Asia, 61. Both sit far above the global median of 50. Government investment in digital infrastructure likely plays a role, but widespread mobile-first adoption matters too.

📊
36

Western Nations Build AI but Don't Trust It

The US scores 36 despite 90 in high-tech adoption. Sweden (36), Belgium (33), and Canada (39) show the same disconnect. Western Europe averages 44; North America, 38. People closer to AI's labor-market effects tend to be warier of it.

🌱
58

Africa: Sparse Data, Real Signals

Rwanda scores 58; South Africa, 63; Kenya, 44. Data coverage is thin — 14 of 21 African countries in the index have low-confidence scores. Still, tech hubs in Nairobi and Kigali point to genuine optimism, particularly around AI for agriculture and public services.

💡
49pt

Adoption Does Not Imply Acceptance

China (82) and Belgium (33) sit 49 points apart. The US has 90 in high-tech adoption but only 36 in acceptance. Taiwan: 86 vs 72. Poland: 81 vs 42. Using AI and welcoming it are plainly different things.

Weighted Scoring Model

We compute each country's score as a weighted average across all available survey and index data. Two factors determine weights: how credible the source is and how recent the data is.

Source Credibility Tiers

  • Very High (weight 1.0): Pew Research Center, Stanford HAI, ITU ICT Index
  • High (weight 0.85): Ipsos Global Advisor, Oxford Insights, IMD World Digital Competitiveness
  • Medium-High (weight 0.7): Edelman Trust Barometer, McKinsey, Standard Insights

Recency Adjustment

  • 2025 data: weight multiplier 1.0
  • 2024 data: weight multiplier 0.9
  • 2023 data: weight multiplier 0.7
  • 2021 data: weight multiplier 0.35 (flagged as pre-ChatGPT historical baseline)

Confidence Levels

High: Nationally representative survey data (Pew, Ipsos, or Edelman).
Medium: Composite indices or smaller-sample surveys from credible organizations.
Low: No direct survey data. Estimated from readiness indices and regional patterns.

Data Quality Notes

  • Lloyd's Register Foundation 2021 and EU AI Watch 2021 data flagged as historical baselines (pre-ChatGPT era)
  • New Zealand IMD duplicate removed (kept rank 27 only)
  • Countries without direct survey data are marked as estimated (is_estimated: true)
